Our Workshops

Navigating Relationships

Help students develop emotional intelligence and effective communication skills to foster healthy relationships.

Goals:

Help students develop emotional intelligence and effective communication skills to foster healthy relationships.

Activities:

  • Icebreaker: Relationship scenarios‚Äîstudents discuss appropriate responses to real-life scenarios like peer pressure, misunderstandings, or conflicts.
  • Role-playing: Practice active listening and conflict resolution in pairs.
  • Discussion: Explore boundaries, trust, and respect in friendships, family, and romantic relationships.
  • Reflection Activity: Write about a relationship they want to improve and steps to take.

Time Management Workshop

Teach students strategies to manage their time effectively and prioritize responsibilities.

Goals:

Teach students strategies to manage their time effectively and prioritize responsibilities.

Activities:

  • Interactive Calendar Exercise: Create a weekly schedule balancing school, hobbies, and relaxation.
  • Group Activity: Time-wasters vs. time-savers‚Äîstudents sort habits into categories and brainstorm alternatives.
  • Simulation Game: Tackle a mock day full of tasks and deadlines using prioritization techniques.
  • Reflection: Write one time-management goal and share an accountability plan.

Resilience & Stress Management: Thriving Under Pressure

Equip students with tools to cope with stress, overcome challenges, and develop a growth mindset.

Goals:

Equip students with tools to cope with stress, overcome challenges, and develop a growth mindset.

Activities:

  • Mindfulness Practice: A guided breathing or meditation exercise.
  • Journaling: Write about a time they overcame a challenge and what they learned.
  • Group Brainstorm: List common stressors and brainstorm healthy coping strategies.
  • Art Activity: Create a "stress relief toolkit" poster with personal coping mechanisms.

Career Exploration: Finding Your Passion

Encourage students to explore their interests, skills, and potential career paths.

Goals:

Encourage students to explore their interests, skills, and potential career paths.

Activities:

  • Career Speed-Dating: Rotate among stations to hear about different professions from mentors or guest speakers.
  • Passion Mapping: Students create a "map" connecting their interests to potential careers.
  • Resume Building: Workshop a simple resume or college application activity for age-appropriate audiences.
  • Goal Setting: Write a short- and long-term goal related to their future career interests.

Digital Well-being: Navigating Technology Wisely

Help students understand the impact of social media and screen time on mental health and productivity.

Goals:

Help students understand the impact of social media and screen time on mental health and productivity.

Activities:

  • Tech Use Audit: Track and analyze daily screen time.
  • Discussion: Explore social media's benefits and downsides, focusing on body image, comparison, and misinformation.
  • Scenario Exercise: Role-play situations like online conflicts or cyberbullying and discussing healthy responses.
  • Commitment Pledge: Create a personal plan for healthy digital habits.

Financial Literacy Basics: Managing Money Wisely

Teach students essential money management skills, including budgeting, saving, and smart spending.

Goals:

Teach students essential money management skills, including budgeting, saving, and smart spending.

Activities:

  • Budget Challenge: Students create a mock budget for a monthly income.
  • Needs vs. Wants Game: Categorize expenses to understand spending priorities.
  • Group Activity: Compare saving and investment strategies through simple simulations.
  • Reflection: Write one financial goal and discuss ways to achieve it.
